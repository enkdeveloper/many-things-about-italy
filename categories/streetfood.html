Street Food Recipes

Arancini

Deep-fried rice balls stuffed with a savory filling, such as ragu, cheese, or peas.

Ingredients:
  • Arborio rice
  • Chicken or vegetable broth
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Eggs
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Tomato sauce (optional)
  • Peas (optional)
  • Ground beef (optional)
  • Fresh parsley
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
Instructions:

1. Cook Arborio rice in chicken or vegetable broth until tender. Let it cool slightly.
2. Stir in grated Parmesan cheese, chopped fresh parsley, and beaten eggs.
3. Take a small handful of the rice mixture and flatten it in your hand. Place a cube of mozzarella cheese and any desired filling (such as peas or ground beef) in the center.
4. Mold the rice mixture around the filling to form a ball.
5. Roll the rice ball in breadcrumbs until coated evenly.
6. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
7. Fry the rice balls in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes.
8. Drain the arancini on paper towels and serve hot with tomato sauce for dipping, if desired.

Piadina

Thin, unleavened Italian flatbread filled with various savory ingredients, such as cheese, cured meats, or vegetables.

Ingredients:
  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking powder
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Water
  • Prosciutto
  • Stracchino cheese
  • Arugula
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)
Instructions:

1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, baking powder, salt, and olive oil.
2. Gradually add water, mixing until a smooth dough forms.
3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a thin circle.
4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Cook each piadina for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until lightly golden and cooked through.
5. Fill each piadina with slices of prosciutto, stracchino cheese, arugula, and halved cherry tomatoes.
6. Fold the piadina in half and serve immediately, optionally drizzled with balsamic glaze.

Panelle

Crispy chickpea fritters seasoned with herbs and spices, typically served as a street food snack in Sicily.

Ingredients:
  • Chickpea flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Parsley
  • Olive oil (for frying)
Instructions:

1. In a saucepan, whisk together chickpea flour, water, salt, black pepper, and chopped parsley until smooth.
2. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened and smooth.
3. Pour the mixture onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, spreading it into an even layer.
4. Let the mixture cool and set for about 1-2 hours.
5. Cut the set chickpea mixture into squares or rectangles.
6. Heat olive oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat.
7. Fry the panelle in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.
8. Drain the panelle on paper towels and serve hot as a snack or appetizer.

Schiacciata

A type of Italian flatbread, similar to focaccia, topped with olive oil, salt, and sometimes herbs.

Ingredients:
  • All-purpose flour
  • Active dry yeast
  • Water
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Rosemary (optional)
Instructions:

1. In a mixing bowl, combine active dry yeast, warm water, and sugar. Let it sit for about 5 minutes, or until frothy.
2. Stir in all-purpose flour, olive oil, and salt until a dough forms.
3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface until smooth and elastic.
4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
5. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to a greased baking sheet.
6. Press the dough into a rectangle shape, about 1/2 inch thick.
7. Brush the surface of the dough with olive oil and sprinkle with coarse salt and fresh rosemary, if desired.
8. Bake the schiacciata in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through.
9. Let the schiacciata cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Fried Zucchini Flowers

Delicate zucchini flowers stuffed with cheese or other fillings, dipped in batter, and deep-fried until crispy.

Ingredients:
  • Zucchini flowers
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh herbs (such as basil or parsley)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Egg
  • Cold water
  • Salt
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
Instructions:

1. Gently rinse the zucchini flowers under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.
2. Carefully remove the stamen from inside each zucchini flower.
3. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped fresh herbs, and salt to taste.
4. Gently stuff each zucchini flower with the cheese mixture, being careful not to tear the petals.
5. In a separate bowl, whisk together all-purpose flour, beaten egg, cold water, and a pinch of salt to make a batter.
6. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
7. Dip each stuffed zucchini flower into the batter, allowing any excess batter to drip off.
8. Fry the zucchini flowers in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.
9. Drain the fried zucchini flowers on paper towels and serve hot, optionally garnished with additional grated Parmesan cheese.

Trapizzino

A popular Roman street food consisting of pizza dough filled with various savory ingredients, such as meatballs, chicken, or eggplant parmigiana.

Ingredients:
  • Pizza dough
  • Marinara sauce
  • Meatballs
  • Chicken cacciatore
  • Eggplant parmigiana
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out pizza dough into small oval shapes, about 6 inches long.
2. Spread a spoonful of marinara sauce onto each piece of dough.
3. Top the marinara sauce with your choice of filling, such as meatballs, chicken cacciatore, or eggplant parmigiana.
4. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese over the filling.
5. Fold the dough over the filling to create a triangular shape, pinching the edges to seal.
6. Place the trapizzini on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
7. Brush the tops of the trapizzini with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and black pepper.
8. Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and cooked through.
9. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before serving.
10. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving, if desired.

Panzerotti

Deep-fried turnovers filled with tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and sometimes additional ingredients like ham or mushrooms.

Ingredients:
  • Pizza dough
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Ham (optional)
  • Mushrooms (optional)
  • Olive oil (for frying)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Roll out pizza dough into small circles, about 5-6 inches in diameter.
2. Spread a spoonful of tomato sauce onto one half of each dough circle, leaving a small border around the edges.
3. Top the tomato sauce with shredded mozzarella cheese and any desired additional fillings, such as diced ham or sliced mushrooms.
4. Fold the other half of the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape, pressing the edges together to seal.
5. Heat olive oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
6. Fry the panzerotti in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
7. Remove the fried panzerotti from the oil and drain on paper towels.
8. Season with salt and black pepper, and serve hot as a delicious street food snack.

Gelato

Italian-style ice cream made with milk, sugar, and various flavorings, such as fruit purees, nuts, or chocolate.

Ingredients:
  • Whole milk
  • Heavy cream
  • Sugar
  • Egg yolks
  • Vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit puree, nuts, or chocolate (optional)
Instructions:

1. In a saucepan, heat whole milk and heavy cream over medium heat until warm but not boiling.
2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together sugar and egg yolks until pale and creamy.
3. Gradually pour the warm milk mixture into the egg mixture, whisking constantly.
4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until thickened.
5. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract.
6. Transfer the custard to a bowl and cover with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly onto the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming.
7. Chill the custard in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.
8. Once chilled, churn the custard in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions.
9. If desired, swirl in fresh fruit puree, chopped nuts, or chocolate during the last few minutes of churning.
10. Transfer the churned gelato to a freezer-safe container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

Zeppole

Deep-fried dough balls dusted with powdered sugar, typically served as a street food snack during Italian festivals and celebrations.

Ingredients:
  • All-purpose flour
  • Sugar
  • Baking powder
  • Salt
  • Eggs
  • Whole milk
  • Vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)
Instructions:

1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
2. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, whole milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.
3. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until a thick batter forms.
4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 375°F (190°C).
5. Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes.
6. Remove the zeppole from the oil with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
7. Dust the warm zeppole generously with powdered sugar.
8. Serve the zeppole immediately as a delightful street food treat.
