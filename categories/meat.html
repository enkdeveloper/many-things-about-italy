Meat Recipes

Osso Buco

Delicious Italian dish made with chicken or veal meat containing bone-in meat pieces.

Ingredients:
  • Chicken or veal meat
  • Flour
  • Olive oil
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Tomato paste
  • White wine
  • Chicken or beef broth
  • Bay leaf
  • Thyme
  • Parsley
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Gremolata (optional)
Instructions:

1. Season the meat with salt and pepper, then dredge in flour.
2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium-high heat.
3. Brown the meat on all sides, then remove from the pan and set aside.
4. In the same pan, add chopped onion, carrot, celery, and garlic. Sauté until softened.
5. Stir in tomato paste and cook for a few minutes.
6. Pour in white wine and deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits.
7. Return the meat to the pan and add chicken or beef broth, bay leaf, thyme, and parsley.
8. Cover and simmer for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender.
9. Serve hot with gremolata, if desired.

Bistecca alla Fiorentina

Grilled and salted large steak.

Ingredients:
  • Large steak (such as T-bone or porterhouse)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil
Instructions:

1. Preheat the grill to high heat.
2. Season the steak generously with salt and pepper.
3. Drizzle with olive oil.
4. Grill the steak for about 5-6 minutes on each side, or until desired doneness.
5. Let the steak rest for a few minutes before slicing.
6. Serve hot.

Pollo alla Cacciatora

Chicken cooked with tomatoes, peppers, and onions.

Ingredients:
  • Chicken pieces
  • Tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Bell pepper
  • Garlic
  • White wine
  • Chicken broth
  • Olive oil
  • Bay leaf
  • Thyme
  • Oregano
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Season the chicken pieces with salt and black pepper.
2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
3. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides, then remove from the pan and set aside.
4. In the same pan, add chopped onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Sauté until softened.
5. Stir in diced tomatoes, white wine, chicken broth, bay leaf, thyme, and oregano.
6. Return the chicken to the pan and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
7. Serve hot.

Saltimbocca alla Romana

Roman dish made with veal, ham, and fresh sage between parchment paper.

Ingredients:
  • Veal scallops
  • Prosciutto
  • Fresh sage leaves
  • Flour
  • Butter
  • White wine
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Season the veal scallops with salt and black pepper, then dredge in flour.
2. Place a slice of prosciutto and a sage leaf on each veal scallop.
3. Secure with toothpicks.
4. In a large pan, melt butter over medium heat.
5. Cook the veal scallops until golden brown on both sides.
6. Pour in white wine and simmer until the sauce is reduced.
7. Serve hot.

Ragu alla Bolognese

Meaty pasta sauce made with tomatoes and spices.

Ingredients:
  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Tomato paste
  • Canned tomatoes
  • Red wine
  • Beef broth
  • Bay leaf
  • Thyme
  • Oregano
  • Basil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. In a large pot, cook chopped onion, carrot, celery, and garlic until softened.
2. Add ground beef and cook until browned.
3. Stir in tomato paste and cook for a few minutes.
4. Pour in red wine and cook until reduced by half.
5. Add canned tomatoes, beef broth, bay leaf, thyme, oregano, and basil.
6. Simmer uncovered for about 1-2 hours, stirring occasionally.
7. Season with salt and black pepper.
8. Serve hot over cooked pasta.

Arrosticini

Italian lamb skewers marinated with olive oil and pepper, then grilled.

Ingredients:
  • Lamb meat
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Cut lamb meat into small pieces and thread onto skewers.
2. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and black pepper.
3. Grill the skewers over medium-high heat for about 5-7 minutes, or until cooked through.
4. Serve hot.

Braciola di Maiale

Pan-seared pork chop cooked with garlic and peppers.

Ingredients:
  • Pork chops
  • Garlic
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Season the pork chops with salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes.
2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium-high heat.
3. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
4. Sear the pork chops on both sides until golden brown and cooked through.
5. Serve hot.

Ossobuco alla Milanese

Milanese-style braised veal shanks cooked with wine, tomatoes, and aromatics.

Ingredients:
  • Veal shanks
  • Flour
  • Olive oil
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Tomatoes
  • White wine
  • Beef broth
  • Bay leaf
  • Thyme
  • Gremolata (optional)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Season the veal shanks with salt and black pepper, then dredge in flour.
2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium-high heat.
3. Brown the veal shanks on all sides, then remove from the pot and set aside.
4. In the same pot, add chopped onion, carrot, celery, and garlic. Sauté until softened.
5. Stir in diced tomatoes and cook for a few minutes.
6. Return the veal shanks to the pot and pour in white wine and beef broth.
7. Add bay leaf and thyme.
8. Cover and simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender.
9. Serve hot with gremolata, if desired.

Fettina di Vitello

Thinly sliced veal coated with flour and egg, then fried.

Ingredients:
  • Veal slices
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Bread crumbs
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil
Instructions:

1. Season the veal slices with salt and black pepper.
2. Dredge the veal slices in flour, then dip in beaten egg.
3. Coat with bread crumbs.
4. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
5. Fry the veal slices until golden brown and cooked through.
6. Serve hot.

Polpette al Sugo

Italian meatballs cooked in tomato sauce and served with parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:
  • Ground beef
  • Bread crumbs
  • Egg
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Garlic
  • Parsley
  • Olive oil
  • Tomato sauce
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. In a large bowl, mix together ground beef, bread crumbs, grated parmesan cheese, minced garlic, chopped parsley, beaten egg, salt, and black pepper.
2. Form the mixture into meatballs.
3. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
4. Brown the meatballs on all sides.
5. Pour in tomato sauce and simmer for about 20-30 minutes.
6. Serve hot with additional grated parmesan cheese.
