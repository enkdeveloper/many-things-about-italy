Culinary Traditions Recipes

Bistecca alla Fiorentina

A classic Florentine dish consisting of a thick T-bone steak, seasoned with salt, pepper, and olive oil, then grilled over an open flame.

Ingredients:
  • T-bone steak
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil
Instructions:

1. Season the T-bone steak generously with salt and black pepper on both sides.
2. Drizzle olive oil over the steak and rub it evenly into the meat.
3. Preheat a grill or grill pan over high heat.
4. Place the steak on the grill and cook for about 5-6 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or adjust cooking time according to desired doneness.
5. Remove the steak from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
6. Slice the steak and serve it hot, drizzled with any juices accumulated during resting.

Pappa al Pomodoro

A traditional Tuscan bread soup made with tomatoes, stale bread, olive oil, garlic, and fresh basil.

Ingredients:
  • Stale bread
  • Tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Fresh basil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
2. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, but not browned.
3. Add chopped tomatoes (fresh or canned) to the pot and cook until they start to break down.
4. Tear stale bread into small pieces and add them to the pot.
5. Stir to combine and let the bread soak up the tomato mixture.
6. Add water or vegetable broth to the pot, enough to cover the bread mixture.
7. Simmer the soup for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened.
8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
9. Remove from heat and stir in chopped fresh basil.
10. Serve hot, drizzled with olive oil and garnished with additional basil leaves.

Focaccia di Recco

A Ligurian specialty consisting of thin layers of dough filled with Stracchino cheese and baked until crispy and golden brown.

Ingredients:
  • Pizza dough
  • Stracchino cheese
  • Olive oil
Instructions:

1. Preheat the oven to the highest temperature setting, usually around 500°F (260°C).
2. Divide pizza dough into two equal portions and roll each portion out into a thin circle.
3. Place one layer of dough on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
4. Spread Stracchino cheese evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
5. Cover the cheese with the second layer of dough and press the edges together to seal.
6. Drizzle olive oil over the top layer of dough.
7. Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-15 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown and crispy.
8. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
9. Serve warm as a delicious appetizer or snack.

Risi e Bisi

A traditional Venetian dish consisting of creamy risotto cooked with fresh peas and flavored with pancetta, onion, and Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:
  • Arborio rice
  • Fresh peas
  • Pancetta
  • Onion
  • Chicken or vegetable broth
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Cook diced pancetta in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy. Remove from skillet and set aside.
2. In the same skillet, sauté diced onion until translucent.
3. Add Arborio rice to the skillet and toast for a few minutes, stirring constantly.
4. Pour in a ladleful of warm chicken or vegetable broth and stir until absorbed by the rice.
5. Continue adding broth, a ladleful at a time, stirring frequently, until the rice is creamy and tender, about 18-20 minutes.
6. Stir in fresh peas and cooked pancetta during the last few minutes of cooking.
7. Remove from heat and stir in grated Parmesan cheese and butter until melted and creamy.
8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
9. Serve hot, garnished with additional grated Parmesan cheese if desired.

Torta Pasqualina

A traditional Ligurian Easter pie made with layers of thin pastry dough filled with a mixture of ricotta cheese, Swiss chard, and eggs.

Ingredients:
  • Pie dough
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Swiss chard
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Nutmeg
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. Prepare the filling by blanching Swiss chard in boiling water for a few minutes, then draining and squeezing out excess moisture.
3. Chop the Swiss chard and sauté it in olive oil until tender. Season with salt, black pepper, and nutmeg.
4. In a large bowl, mix together ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, and beaten eggs. Add the cooked Swiss chard and mix until well combined.
5. Roll out the pie dough and line a pie dish with it, leaving some overhang.
6. Spread the ricotta and Swiss chard mixture evenly over the dough.
7. Make small wells in the filling and carefully crack an egg into each well.
8. Fold the overhanging dough over the filling, creating a rustic edge.
9. Brush the dough with beaten egg wash for a golden finish.
10. Bake in the preheated oven for about 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set.
11. Allow the pie to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Pasta e Ceci

A simple yet flavorful Roman dish consisting of pasta and chickpeas cooked in a tomato-based broth.

Ingredients:
  • Ditalini or other small pasta
  • Canned chickpeas
  • Tomato sauce
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Vegetable broth
  • Rosemary
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened.
2. Stir in tomato sauce, canned chickpeas (rinsed and drained), vegetable broth, chopped rosemary, and red pepper flakes.
3. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
4. Add dried pasta to the pot and continue to simmer until the pasta is al dente, stirring occasionally.
5. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
6. Serve hot, garnished with a drizzle of olive oil and grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.

Ossobuco alla Milanese

A traditional Milanese dish consisting of cross-cut veal shanks braised with vegetables, white wine, and broth, served with gremolata.

Ingredients:
  • Veal shanks
  • Flour
  • Olive oil
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • White wine
  • Beef or veal broth
  • Canned tomatoes
  • Gremolata (lemon zest, garlic, parsley)
Instructions:

1. Season veal shanks with salt and pepper, then dredge in flour.
2. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown veal shanks on both sides, then remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, sauté diced onion, carrot, and celery until softened.
4. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
5. Deglaze the pot with white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
6. Return the veal shanks to the pot and add beef or veal broth, canned tomatoes (with juices), and any accumulated juices from the veal.
7. Bring the liquid to a simmer, then cover and reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer gently for about 2-3 hours, or until the veal is tender and falling off the bone.
8. Meanwhile, prepare the gremolata by combining minced garlic, lemon zest, and chopped parsley.
9. Serve the ossobuco hot, garnished with gremolata, and accompanied by risotto or polenta.

Carciofi alla Romana

Artichokes cooked Roman-style, braised with garlic, fresh herbs, and white wine until tender and flavorful.

Ingredients:
  • Artichokes
  • Lemon
  • Garlic
  • Parsley
  • Mint
  • White wine
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Prepare the artichokes by trimming off the tough outer leaves and cutting off the top third of each artichoke. Rub cut surfaces with lemon to prevent browning.
2. In a bowl, mix together chopped garlic, parsley, mint, salt, and black pepper.
3. Stuff the garlic and herb mixture between the leaves of each artichoke.
4. Place stuffed artichokes in a deep pot or Dutch oven, stem side down.
5. Drizzle olive oil over the artichokes and pour white wine into the pot until it reaches about halfway up the artichokes.
6. Cover the pot and cook over low heat for about 30-40 minutes, or until the artichokes are tender when pierced with a fork.
7. Serve the artichokes hot, drizzled with cooking juices and garnished with additional chopped herbs if desired.

Sgabei

Light and airy fried bread dough squares, typically stuffed with cheese, prosciutto, or other savory fillings, originating from the Italian Riviera.

Ingredients:
  • All-purpose flour
  • Yeast
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Cheese, prosciutto, or other savory fillings (optional)
Instructions:

1. In a bowl, combine all-purpose flour, yeast, water, and salt to make a smooth dough.
2. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm place until doubled in size, about 1-2 hours.
3. Punch down the dough and divide it into small pieces.
4. Roll each piece of dough into a thin square or rectangle.
5. Heat olive oil in a deep skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat.
6. Fry the dough squares in hot oil until puffed and golden brown on both sides.
7. Remove the sgabei from the oil and drain on paper towels.
8. If desired, slice open the sgabei and stuff them with cheese, prosciutto, or other savory fillings.
9. Serve the sgabei hot as a delicious appetizer or snack.

Pasta alla Norma

A Sicilian pasta dish made with sautéed eggplant, tomato sauce, basil, and ricotta salata cheese.

Ingredients:
  • Pasta (such as spaghetti or penne)
  • Eggplant
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Basil
  • Olive oil
  • Ricotta salata cheese
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Cook pasta in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
2. Meanwhile, cut eggplant into cubes and sprinkle with salt. Let it sit for about 20-30 minutes to release excess moisture.
3. Rinse the eggplant cubes and pat them dry with paper towels.
4. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
5. Add eggplant cubes to the skillet and cook until golden brown and tender.
6. Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and form a sauce.
7. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
8. Add cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat it in the sauce.
9. Tear fresh basil leaves and add them to the pasta, stirring until evenly distributed.
10. Serve hot, garnished with grated ricotta salata cheese and additional basil leaves if desired.
