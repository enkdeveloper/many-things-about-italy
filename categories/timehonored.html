Time Honored Recipes

Parmigiana di Melanzane

Layers of fried eggplant slices, tomato sauce, and mozzarella cheese, baked to perfection.

Ingredients:
  • Eggplant
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Basil leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
Instructions:

1. Slice the eggplant and sprinkle with salt. Let it sit for 30 minutes, then rinse and pat dry.
2. Fry the eggplant slices until golden brown.
3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
4. In a baking dish, layer the fried eggplant slices with tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, parmesan cheese, and basil leaves.
5. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of cheese on top.
6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
7. Let it cool slightly before serving.

Pappa al Pomodoro

A classic Tuscan bread and tomato soup, enriched with olive oil, garlic, and basil.

Ingredients:
  • Stale bread
  • Tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Basil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
Instructions:

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
2. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and release their juices.
3. Tear the stale bread into small pieces and add them to the pot. Stir to combine.
4. Add water or vegetable broth to reach the desired consistency.
5. Season with salt and pepper, then simmer for about 20-25 minutes.
6. Stir in chopped basil and cook for another 5 minutes.
7. Serve hot, drizzled with extra olive oil and garnished with fresh basil leaves.

Osso Buco alla Milanese

A traditional Milanese dish featuring braised veal shanks cooked with white wine, broth, tomatoes, and aromatics.

Ingredients:
  • Veal shanks
  • Flour
  • Butter
  • Olive oil
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Tomatoes
  • White wine
  • Beef or chicken broth
  • Bay leaf
  • Thyme
  • Gremolata (optional, for serving)
Instructions:

1. Season the veal shanks with salt and pepper, then dredge them in flour.
2. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat butter and olive oil over medium-high heat.
3. Add the veal shanks and brown them on all sides. Remove and set aside.
4. In the same pot, add diced onion, carrot, and celery. Sauté until softened.
5. Stir in minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
6. Add crushed tomatoes, white wine, broth, bay leaf, and thyme. Bring to a simmer.
7. Return the veal shanks to the pot, cover, and cook over low heat for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender.
8. Serve hot, garnished with gremolata if desired.

Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Roman pasta dish made with spaghetti, eggs, pancetta, pecorino cheese, and black pepper.

Ingredients:
  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Pancetta or guanciale
  • Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
Instructions:

1. Cook spaghetti in boiling salted water until al dente.
2. Meanwhile, in a skillet, cook diced pancetta until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated pecorino cheese, and black pepper.
4. Drain the cooked spaghetti, reserving some of the pasta water.
5. Immediately toss the hot spaghetti with the egg and cheese mixture, allowing the heat from the pasta to cook the eggs and melt the cheese.
6. Add the cooked pancetta and toss to combine. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of pasta water.
7. Season with salt and extra black pepper if desired.
8. Serve hot, garnished with additional grated pecorino cheese.

Panzanella

A traditional Tuscan salad made with stale bread, tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, basil, and a tangy vinaigrette.

Ingredients:
  • Stale bread
  • Tomatoes
  • Cucumber
  • Red onion
  • Basil
  • Red wine vinegar
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
Instructions:

1. Tear the stale bread into bite-sized pieces and place them in a large bowl.
2. Chop the tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion into similar-sized pieces and add them to the bowl.
3. Tear fresh basil leaves and add them to the salad.
4. In a small bowl, whisk together red wine vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper to make the vinaigrette.
5. Pour the vinaigrette over the salad and toss gently to combine.
6. Let the salad sit for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
7. Serve at room temperature, garnished with additional basil leaves if desired.

Ragu alla Bolognese

A rich and hearty meat sauce made with ground beef, tomatoes, onions, carrots, celery, and wine, served over pasta.

Ingredients:
  • Ground beef
  • Tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Red wine
  • Beef broth
  • Tomato paste
  • Bay leaf
  • Thyme
  • Rosemary
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
Instructions:

1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion, carrot, and celery, and sauté until softened.
2. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
3. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
4. Stir in tomato paste and cook for a few minutes.
5. Deglaze the pot with red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
6. Add chopped tomatoes, beef broth, bay leaf, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
7. Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.
8. Serve hot over cooked pasta, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese.

Linguine alle Vongole

Linguine pasta served with fresh clams, garlic, white wine, parsley, and chili flakes, a classic Italian seafood dish.

Ingredients:
  • Linguine pasta
  • Clams
  • Garlic
  • White wine
  • Olive oil
  • Parsley
  • Red chili flakes
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Cook linguine pasta in salted boiling water until al dente.
2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and chili flakes, and sauté until fragrant.
3. Add fresh clams to the skillet and pour in white wine.
4. Cover the skillet and let the clams steam until they open, about 5-7 minutes.
5. Remove the cooked clams from the skillet and set aside.
6. Add cooked linguine to the skillet with the clam cooking liquid. Toss to coat.
7. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
8. Serve the linguine topped with steamed clams and chopped parsley.

Tiramisu

A classic Italian dessert made with layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cheese, and cocoa powder.

Ingredients:
  • Ladyfingers
  • Espresso coffee
  • Mascarpone cheese
  • Egg yolks
  • Sugar
  • Marsala wine
  • Cocoa powder
Instructions:

1. Brew strong espresso coffee and let it cool.
2. In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy.
3. Add mascarpone cheese and marsala wine to the egg mixture. Beat until smooth.
4. Dip ladyfingers in the cooled coffee and arrange them in a single layer in a serving dish.
5. Spread a layer of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
6. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of mascarpone mixture on top.
7. Cover and refrigerate the tiramisu for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to set.
8. Before serving, dust the top of the tiramisu with cocoa powder.

Caprese Salad

A simple Italian salad made with fresh tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, basil leaves, olive oil, and balsamic glaze.

Ingredients:
  • Tomatoes
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Slice the tomatoes and fresh mozzarella cheese into similar-sized slices.
2. Arrange the tomato and mozzarella slices on a serving platter, alternating and overlapping them.
3. Tuck fresh basil leaves between the tomato and mozzarella slices.
4. Drizzle extra-virgin olive oil and balsamic glaze over the salad.
5. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
6. Serve immediately as a refreshing appetizer or side dish.

Amatriciana Pasta

A classic Roman pasta dish made with bucatini pasta, pancetta, tomatoes, onions, pecorino cheese, and chili flakes.

Ingredients:
  • Bucatini pasta
  • Pancetta
  • Tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Red chili flakes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Cook bucatini pasta in salted boiling water until al dente.
2. Meanwhile, in a skillet, cook diced pancetta in olive oil until crispy. Remove from skillet and set aside.
3. In the same skillet, sauté diced onion until translucent.
4. Add crushed tomatoes and red chili flakes to the skillet. Simmer for about 10 minutes.
5. Drain the cooked pasta and add it to the skillet with the tomato sauce.
6. Toss to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce.
7. Stir in the cooked pancetta and grated pecorino cheese.
8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
9. Serve hot, garnished with additional grated pecorino cheese if desired.

Panzarotti

Deep-fried mini calzones filled with tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and sometimes additional ingredients like pepperoni or ham.

Ingredients:
  • Pizza dough
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pepperoni or ham (optional)
Instructions:

1. Divide pizza dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.
2. Flatten each ball into a small disc and place a spoonful of tomato sauce and a piece of mozzarella cheese in the center.
3. Add additional toppings like pepperoni or ham if desired.
4. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape and seal the edges tightly.
5. Heat olive oil in a deep fryer or large skillet to 350°F (175°C).
6. Fry the panzarotti in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
7. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
8. Serve hot as a delicious snack or appetizer.

Cacio e Pepe

A simple Roman pasta dish made with spaghetti, Pecorino Romano cheese, black pepper, and pasta water.

Ingredients:
  • Spaghetti
  • Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
Instructions:

1. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente.
2. Meanwhile, grate Pecorino Romano cheese and coarsely grind black pepper.
3. Reserve some pasta water, then drain the cooked spaghetti.
4. In a skillet, toss the hot spaghetti with grated Pecorino Romano cheese and black pepper.
5. Add a splash of pasta water and continue tossing until the cheese melts and forms a creamy sauce.
6. Season with salt to taste.
7. Serve hot, garnished with extra grated cheese and black pepper.

Zuppa di Pesce

A traditional Italian seafood soup made with a variety of fish and shellfish, tomatoes, wine, and aromatics.

Ingredients:
  • Assorted fish and shellfish (such as shrimp, clams, mussels, squid, and white fish fillets)
  • Tomatoes
  • White wine
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Fish or vegetable broth
  • Fresh herbs (such as parsley, basil, and thyme)
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Clean and prepare the seafood, removing shells, deveining shrimp, and cutting fish into bite-sized pieces.
2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened.
3. Add chopped tomatoes, white wine, fish or vegetable broth, and red pepper flakes to the pot. Bring to a simmer.
4. Add the seafood to the pot, starting with the clams and mussels, as they take longer to cook.
5. Cover and simmer until the clams and mussels begin to open, then add the remaining seafood and fresh herbs.
6. Continue to simmer until all the seafood is cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
7. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
8. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs and crusty bread on the side.

Gnocchi alla Sorrentina

Soft potato dumplings baked with tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil, a classic Neapolitan dish.

Ingredients:
  • Potatoes
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Boil potatoes until tender, then peel and mash them while still warm.
2. Add flour, egg, salt, and pepper to the mashed potatoes, and mix until a dough forms.
3. Roll the dough into ropes, then cut into bite-sized pieces.
4. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil, then cook the gnocchi in batches until they float to the surface.
5. Drain the cooked gnocchi and transfer them to a baking dish.
6. Pour tomato sauce over the gnocchi, then top with slices of fresh mozzarella cheese and torn basil leaves.
7. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.
8. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
9. Serve hot, garnished with additional fresh basil leaves.
