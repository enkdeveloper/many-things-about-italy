Family Favorites Recipes

Nonna's Lasagna

A classic family favorite, featuring layers of pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and gooey mozzarella cheese.

Ingredients:
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Italian sausage
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Red wine
  • Olive oil
  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Butter
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Nutmeg
Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened.
4. Add ground beef and Italian sausage to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon, and cook until browned.
5. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, and red wine. Simmer for about 20 minutes, then season with salt and black pepper to taste.
6. In a separate saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Stir in flour to create a roux, then gradually whisk in milk until smooth. Cook until thickened, then season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
7. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Top with a layer of lasagna noodles, followed by a layer of béchamel sauce and shredded mozzarella cheese.
8. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of béchamel and shredded Parmesan cheese on top.
9. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for about 30 minutes.
10. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown. Let it cool slightly before serving.

Grandma's Meatballs

Juicy and flavorful meatballs made with a blend of ground beef, pork, and veal, simmered in a rich tomato sauce.

Ingredients:
  • Ground beef
  • Ground pork
  • Ground veal
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Egg
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh basil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, ground pork, ground veal, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, beaten egg, finely chopped onion, minced garlic, chopped fresh basil, salt, and black pepper.
3. Mix until well combined, then shape the mixture into meatballs of desired size.
4. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Brown the meatballs on all sides, then transfer them to a baking dish.
5. Pour tomato sauce over the meatballs, covering them completely.
6. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for about 30 minutes.
7. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the meatballs are cooked through and the sauce is bubbling.
8. Serve the meatballs hot, garnished with additional grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves if desired.

Zia Maria's Eggplant Parmesan

Layers of crispy breaded eggplant slices, marinara sauce, and melted mozzarella cheese, baked until golden and bubbly.

Ingredients:
  • Eggplant
  • All-purpose flour
  • Eggs
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Marinara sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh basil
Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish with olive oil.
2. Peel the eggplant and slice it into 1/4-inch rounds.
3. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes: one containing all-purpose flour, one containing beaten eggs, and one containing breadcrumbs mixed with grated Parmesan cheese.
4. Dredge each eggplant slice in flour, then dip it into the beaten eggs, and finally coat it with the breadcrumb mixture.
5. Place the breaded eggplant slices on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and black pepper.
6. Bake the eggplant slices in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.
7. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce in the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
8. Arrange a layer of baked eggplant slices over the sauce, followed by a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese and torn fresh basil leaves.
9. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of marinara sauce and shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
10. Bake the eggplant Parmesan in the oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Let it cool slightly before serving.

Aunt Lucia's Stuffed Peppers

Bell peppers stuffed with a savory mixture of ground meat, rice, tomatoes, and herbs, baked until tender and delicious.

Ingredients:
  • Bell peppers
  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Cooked rice
  • Canned diced tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Fresh parsley
  • Fresh basil
  • Olive oil
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish with olive oil.
2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes from the inside.
3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened.
4. Add ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
5. Stir in cooked rice, canned diced tomatoes, tomato paste, chopped fresh parsley, chopped fresh basil, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper. Cook for a few minutes to combine flavors.
6. Stuff the bell peppers with the meat and rice mixture, pressing it down gently to fill each pepper completely.
7. Place the stuffed peppers in the prepared baking dish. If desired, sprinkle extra Parmesan cheese on top.
8. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for about 30-40 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.
9. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
10. Serve the stuffed peppers hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves if desired.

Cousin Luigi's Chicken Parmesan

Tender chicken breasts coated in seasoned breadcrumbs, fried until golden brown, topped with marinara sauce and melted mozzarella cheese.

Ingredients:
  • Chicken breasts
  • All-purpose flour
  • Eggs
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Marinara sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh parsley
Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish with olive oil.
2. Pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness between two pieces of plastic wrap.
3. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes: one containing all-purpose flour, one containing beaten eggs, and one containing breadcrumbs mixed with grated Parmesan cheese.
4. Season the chicken breasts with salt and black pepper, then dredge each piece in flour, dip it into the beaten eggs, and coat it with the breadcrumb mixture.
5. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the breaded chicken breasts until golden brown and cooked through, about 3-4 minutes per side.
6. Transfer the fried chicken breasts to the prepared baking dish.
7. Spoon marinara sauce over each chicken breast, covering them completely.
8. Top each chicken breast with shredded mozzarella cheese and sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley.
9. Bake the chicken Parmesan in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
10. Serve the chicken Parmesan hot, garnished with additional chopped parsley if desired.

Grandpa Mario's Beef Stew

A hearty and comforting stew made with tender chunks of beef, potatoes, carrots, onions, and savory broth.

Ingredients:
  • Stewing beef
  • Potatoes
  • Carrots
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Beef broth
  • Red wine
  • Tomato paste
  • Bay leaves
  • Fresh thyme
  • Olive oil
  • All-purpose flour
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C).
2. Cut the stewing beef into bite-sized pieces and season with salt and black pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the seasoned beef and cook until browned on all sides.
4. Remove the beef from the Dutch oven and set aside. Add diced onion and minced garlic to the pot, and sauté until softened.
5. Sprinkle flour over the onions and garlic, stirring to coat. Cook for a few minutes to remove the raw flour taste.
6. Deglaze the pot with red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
7. Return the browned beef to the pot, along with diced potatoes, sliced carrots, beef broth, tomato paste, bay leaves, and fresh thyme.
8. Bring the stew to a simmer, then cover the Dutch oven and transfer it to the preheated oven.
9. Cook the stew in the oven for about 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
10. Remove the bay leaves and thyme sprigs before serving. Serve the beef stew hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Sister Maria's Tiramisu

A classic Italian dessert made with layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers and creamy mascarpone cheese, dusted with cocoa powder.

Ingredients:
  • Ladyfingers
  • Espresso
  • Mascarpone cheese
  • Eggs
  • Sugar
  • Vanilla extract
  • Cocoa powder
Instructions:

1. Brew strong espresso and let it cool to room temperature.
2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy.
3. Add mascarpone cheese and vanilla extract to the egg yolk mixture, and beat until smooth and well combined.
4. In a separate bowl, beat egg whites until stiff peaks form.
5. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the mascarpone mixture until no white streaks remain.
6. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the cooled espresso, making sure not to soak them for too long.
7. Arrange a layer of soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of a serving dish.
8. Spread a layer of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
9. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of mascarpone mixture on top.
10. Cover the tiramisu with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to set.
11. Before serving, dust the top of the tiramisu with cocoa powder. Cut into slices and serve chilled.

Uncle Giuseppe's Spaghetti Carbonara

A simple yet delicious pasta dish made with spaghetti, crispy pancetta, eggs, cheese, and black pepper.

Ingredients:
  • Spaghetti
  • Pancetta
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
Instructions:

1. Cook spaghetti in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the spaghetti and set aside.
2. In a skillet, cook diced pancetta over medium heat until crispy. Remove from the skillet and drain on paper towels.
3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, and black pepper.
4. Return the cooked spaghetti to the pot. Add the cooked pancetta and egg mixture, tossing well to coat the spaghetti.
5. If the spaghetti seems dry, add some of the reserved pasta cooking water to loosen it up.
6. Season the spaghetti carbonara with salt to taste, if needed.
7. Serve the spaghetti carbonara hot, garnished with extra grated Parmesan cheese and black pepper.

Aunt Rosa's Cannoli

Crispy pastry shells filled with a sweet and creamy ricotta cheese filling, studded with chocolate chips and candied fruit.

Ingredients:
  • Cannoli shells
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Powdered sugar
  • Vanilla extract
  • Chocolate chips
  • Candied fruit
  • Whipped cream
  • Pistachios
  • Confectioners' sugar
Instructions:

1. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Beat until smooth and creamy.
2. Stir in chocolate chips and chopped candied fruit until evenly distributed.
3. Fill each cannoli shell with the ricotta cheese mixture, using a piping bag or spoon.
4. Dip the ends of the filled cannoli into whipped cream and chopped pistachios.
5. Dust the cannoli with confectioners' sugar just before serving.
6. Serve the cannoli immediately to prevent the shells from becoming soggy.

Mom's Chicken Cacciatore

A rustic Italian chicken stew made with chicken pieces, tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, and aromatic herbs.

Ingredients:
  • Chicken pieces
  • Onion
  • Bell peppers
  • Garlic
  • Canned diced tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Dry white wine
  • Chicken broth
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Fresh thyme
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
Instructions:

1. Season chicken pieces with salt and black pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides, then remove them from the pot and set aside.
3. In the same pot, sauté diced onion, sliced bell peppers, and minced garlic until softened.
4. Stir in canned diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dry white wine, and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer.
5. Return the browned chicken pieces to the pot, along with fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs.
6. Cover the pot and simmer gently for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
7. Adjust seasoning with salt and black pepper to taste.
8. Serve the chicken cacciatore hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.
